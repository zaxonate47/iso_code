Synopsis

The annual cultural fest of the MSU Indian Students Organization, Sargam has grown from being a small event taking place within the confines of he MSU campus to a musical/histrionic extravaganza that the MSU community looks forward to year after year

This year, Sargam takes the historical route, drawing inspiration from one of India's most ancient literary works, Vethala Panchavimshanti (Sanskrit: The twenty five tales of the ghost). Vikram-Vetal follows the journey of the titular King Vikram as he strives to capture a Vetal (Sanskrit: Ghost). in order to fulfill a promise he made to a hermit.

The wily Vetal he eventually encounters isn't about to give in so easily and makes a strange proposition to the mighty King - The Vetal shall narrate a story and if the King utters even a single word, he has to sit through another story; as each story concludes, the King realises that reining in his ego is a mightier task than reining in a thousand ghosts!

Sargam 2011 is an amalgamation of Indian culture, tradition and some good ol'fashioned humor. All are welcome!

Information

Sargam is the annual cultural fest of the Michigan State University, Indian Students Organization (MSUISO). The MSUISO is an organization composed of Indian students from Michigan State University and is a Registered Student Organization (RSO) affiliated to the International Students Association(ISA). The current executive board can be reached at msuic@msu.edu
